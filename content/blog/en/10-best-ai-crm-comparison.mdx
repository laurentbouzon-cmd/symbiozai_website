## Methodology

This comparison covers the 10 CRMs that genuinely integrate AI into their product core in 2026 — not those that added a "generate an email" button as decoration. Criteria evaluated: AI depth, commercial adoption, feature-to-price ratio, and relevance for European SME and mid-market sales teams.

We are SymbiozAI — one of the tools reviewed. We made the effort to be honest about our strengths and limitations, as with everyone else. To understand the categories we use in this comparison, our article [AI-Native CRM vs Traditional CRM](/en/blog/ai-native-crm-vs-traditional-crm) explains why this divide is the right analytical lens.

---

## 1. Salesforce + Agentforce

**Profile**: The market titan. 150,000 customers, massive ecosystem, Agentforce as an autonomous agent platform.

**Strengths**: Extreme customization, unlimited integrations, enterprise market well-addressed. Agentforce enables building complex AI workflows.

**Limitations**: Prohibitive complexity for SMEs. Implementation = months. High TCO. The AI remains a layer on top of 25-year-old architecture.

**Best for**: Large enterprises with a dedicated IT team and a comfortable implementation budget.

**Price**: From €165/user/month (Salesforce Core). Agentforce billed per conversation.

---

## 2. HubSpot + Breeze AI

**Profile**: The SME favorite for 10 years. Breeze is its integrated AI assistant.

**Strengths**: Excellent onboarding, coherent marketing + CRM ecosystem, Breeze for emails, summaries, contact enrichment.

**Limitations**: Breeze is a feature, not an architecture. The CRM remains form-driven. Advanced plans get expensive fast.

**Best for**: SMEs with an integrated marketing/sales team that wants an accessible all-in-one tool.

**Price**: Free (limited) up to €90+/user/month for Pro plans.

---

## 3. Pipedrive + AI Sales Assistant

**Profile**: The visual pipeline benchmark. The AI Sales Assistant suggests priority actions.

**Strengths**: Among the best UX on the market, very visual pipeline, fast onboarding, accessible price.

**Limitations**: The AI stays at the suggestion level — it doesn't act. Manual entry remains necessary. Limited for complex sales cycles.

**Best for**: Sales teams of 5 to 30 people who want a clear pipeline without complexity.

**Price**: From €14/user/month.

---

## 4. Freshsales (Freshworks) + Freddy AI

**Profile**: CRM suite with Freddy AI as integrated assistant. Strong in India and international mid-market.

**Strengths**: Freddy AI for lead scoring, deal predictions, email suggestions. Good price-to-value ratio.

**Limitations**: Less known in Europe. Less rich integration ecosystem than Salesforce or HubSpot. Freddy remains predictive — not agentic.

**Best for**: SMEs/mid-market looking for a feature-to-price balance without Salesforce complexity.

**Price**: From €11/user/month.

---

## 5. Zoho CRM + Zia

**Profile**: The complete ecosystem. Zia is Zoho's AI: scoring, anomalies, predictions.

**Strengths**: Very complete suite (CRM, marketing, support, finance in one ecosystem), competitive price, Zia relevant on scoring and pipeline anomaly detection.

**Limitations**: Dated UX. Zia's AI remains analytical — it doesn't understand natural language or act autonomously.

**Best for**: Mid-market companies seeking a CRM/ERP ecosystem without Salesforce costs.

**Price**: From €14/user/month.

---

## 6. Clay

**Profile**: Not a traditional CRM — an AI-first enrichment and outreach tool. Heavily adopted by growth teams.

**Strengths**: Ultra-powerful lead enrichment (100+ sources), waterfall logic, AI-driven email personalization at individual level. Revolutionary for prospecting.

**Limitations**: Not a CRM (no pipeline, no account management). Must be paired with a CRM. Learning curve.

**Best for**: Outbound and growth teams that want to personalize at scale.

**Price**: From $149/month.

---

## 7. Folk

**Profile**: Lightweight, modern CRM with AI enrichment and multi-purpose relationship management.

**Strengths**: Elegant user experience, automatic contact enrichment, suited for teams managing complex relationships (partnerships, investors, recruiting).

**Limitations**: Limited for structured sales cycles. Lacks pipeline depth and reporting.

**Best for**: Founders, business developers, teams managing a network more than a linear pipeline.

**Price**: From €20/user/month.

---

## 8. Attio

**Profile**: Next-generation CRM with automatic enrichment and customizable views.

**Strengths**: Very polished UX, automatic contact/company enrichment, flexibility of views and workflows. Strong in the startup/tech ecosystem.

**Limitations**: Still young — some enterprise features are missing. AI is more enrichment than autonomy.

**Best for**: Startups and scale-ups seeking a modern CRM without Salesforce rigidity.

**Price**: Free up to 5 members, then $29/user/month.

---

## 9. Microsoft Dynamics 365 + Copilot

**Profile**: The natural option for companies already in the Microsoft ecosystem.

**Strengths**: Native Teams/Outlook/Azure integration, Copilot for summaries, suggestions, analysis. Robust for enterprise.

**Limitations**: Significant implementation complexity. Only relevant if you're Microsoft-centric. The AI remains supplementary, not architectural.

**Best for**: Large enterprises with an established Microsoft ecosystem.

**Price**: From $65/user/month.
